Typhoon Mitigation Tips

Here are some ways to mitigate the effects of a typhoon:

1. Secure Your Property

Ensure that loose objects in your yard are secured or brought indoors. Trim trees and branches that could potentially fall on your property during strong winds.

2. Reinforce Doors and Windows

Install storm shutters or board up windows to protect them from flying debris. Reinforce garage doors to prevent them from buckling under pressure.

3. Stock Up on Supplies

Have an emergency kit ready with essential supplies such as food, water, flashlights, batteries, first aid supplies, and medications. Ensure you have enough supplies to last several days.

4. Create a Family Emergency Plan

Develop a family emergency plan that includes evacuation routes, meeting points, and communication methods. Practice the plan with your family members so everyone knows what to do in an emergency.

5. Stay Informed

Monitor weather updates from reliable sources and heed warnings and evacuation orders issued by local authorities. Stay indoors during the storm and only venture out when it is safe to do so.
